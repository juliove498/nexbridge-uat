Who We Are

NEXBRIDGE is a regulated issuer of digital assets structured with an ETF-like design, based on published rules, and issued natively on Bitcoin's Liquid Network. Each token includes a public term sheet defining fees, rules, risks, and investor rights with dedicated registration numbers.

Like traditional ETFs, NEXBRIDGE tokens track reference markets under clear, published rules. They offer transparency through documented rescaling mechanisms, disclosed fees, fair NAV-based subscriptions/redemptions, regulatory clarity at the token level, proof of reserves, institutional settlement rails, and liquidity focus through OTC and selective exchange listings.

Duly authorized by El Salvador's National Commission of Digital Assets (CNAD), under the registration number EAD-0005. Each issuance receives unique registration numbers. Permissions include issuer licensing, digital asset exchange, platform placement, and investment product management.

Liquid Network provides Bitcoin-anchored assurance, policy-based controls for regulated issuance, predictable efficient settlement, mature security models, and confidential-transaction capabilities reducing front-running risks while preserving verifiability.

Currently monthly PoR attestations by Grant Thornton verify reserves and reconciliations. Phase 2 adds hybrid PoR combining monthly attestations with automatic on-chain verification of custody accounts and blockchain addresses.

Independent, regulated custodians hold segregated, issuance-specific reserves. Controls include dual-control on NAV and mint/burn operations, documented time-stamped approvals, daily reconciliations, and monthly independent assurance attestations.

The canonical issuance exists on Bitcoin technology as the single source of truth. Future access wrappers on other chains would mirror the same RID rules, maintain 1:1 supply ratios, and settle back to the canonical chain.

How Can You Buy

Primary market access targets professional/institutional investors (funds, banks, brokers, family offices) requiring KYC/AML and address whitelisting. Secondary market access for self-directed users in eligible jurisdictions depends on exchange-specific rules and jurisdiction restrictions.

Complete KYC/AML verification per the issuance's RID, register settlement addresses for whitelisting, sign OTC terms, and receive desk contacts and cut-off times before subscription/redemption capabilities activate.

Phase 1 (current): Pre-fund orders, execute at end-of-day NAV with T+1 token activation. Redemptions lock tokens at T and process payouts T+1 at NAV. Phase 2 (upcoming): RFQ for larger requests and optional collateralized margin wallets supporting faster settlement.

Tokens remain in either self-custody through approved whitelisted addresses (like Blockstream Green/Jade) or exchange custody at regulated CEXs listing the issuance. Transfers only occur between approved addresses.

OTC fees appear in OTC Desk T&Cs and each issuance's RID; exchange fees vary by venue with spreads depending on order-book liquidity. No hidden costs; all rules and fees are published in the RID and venue fee schedules.
